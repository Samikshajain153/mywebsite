A Nature Lover's Paradise

Infinity Resorts Corbett, a luxurious nature resort occupying 22 acres , is situated on the fringe of the world renowned Corbett National Park – India’s first and the finest tiger reserve. Amongst the longest running properties in Corbett, Infinity Resort's Golghar - or "Round House" - has become the centrepiece of lodge design in the region. Still standing proudly 28 years later, the Golghar draws yet more guests into its cosy, old-world interior. Infinity Resorts Corbett - nestled amongst the foothills of the Himalayas, the mountain ranges, lush green forests and the Kosi River - will absolutely take your breath away.

Guests at our Corbett resort can relax in a world of calm and serenity while those who feel that tracking wildlife is the priority of their “to do” list – there is no greater excitement than experiencing the adrenaline rush of tracking a tiger on a jeep safari. Animals, such as spotted deer, Sambhar, Rhesus Macaque, Langur monkeys, Elephant, and wild Boar are commonly seen, though Tigers can be spotted with some patience and luck. Of course, the more frequent your safaris are, the better chance of seeing the Lord of the Jungle – the Tiger!

Comfort by the Kosi River

Infinity Resorts Corbett, offers a choice of 50 rooms, all en suite with 2 categories of these rooms in Corbett. They are River-facing and Garden-facing rooms. All these rooms in Corbett have private balconies either with stunning views of the beautiful landscaped mango orchards, cultivated gardens or with Himalayan mountain ranges and Kosi River.

These rooms are luxuriously furnished and appointed to provide guests with comfort and functionality. They are all located throughout the extended garden and orchard. All rooms have either double or twin beds

Corbett National Park - The Animal Home

Corbett National Park which celebrated its 80th Anniversary in 2016, is nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas. Corbett Tiger Reserve lies mainly in the districts of Nainital, Almora and Pauri Garhwal. The reserve, extending over the Terai and Bhabar tracts in Uttarakhand, covers a total area of 1218.54 sq km and constitutes of Corbett National Park (520.82 sq km), Sonanadi Wildlife Sanctuary (301.18 sq km) and Kalagarh Reserve Forest (496.54 sq km).

This National Park near our Uttarakhand resort, has some of its most famous inhabitants that include Mammals such as Tiger, Asian Elephant, Leopard, Sloth Bear, Goral, Chital, Sambar, Barking Deer, Hog Deer, Indian Porcupine, Yellow-throated Marten, and Himalayan Langur. Reptiles such as Mugger Crocodile, Gharial Crocodile, King Cobra and Rock Python; Birds such as Kingfishers, Wagtails, Forktails, Pheasants, Hornbills, Eagles, Vultures, and Storks in addition to several species of migratory waterfowl.
